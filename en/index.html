Manifesto

Why does Aldea exist?

A dignified life instead of constant rush

In Aldea, the goal is not to maximise GDP but to maximise quality of life: time for loved ones, healthy food, space for art, rest, development, experiments, and mistakes ‚Äî all the things the traditional system often does not allow.

Self-sufficiency instead of dependency

The settlement aims for as much independence in energy, food, and infrastructure as possible. Not to isolate itself from the world, but to cooperate with it on equal terms.

Technology serving people

In Aldea, technology is not meant to replace human relationships ‚Äî it is meant to relieve people from what is repetitive, heavy, or boring, so more energy is left for what is truly human.

Pillars

Areas of technology and practice

Energy

Microgrids, energy storage, load management, usage prioritisation ‚Äî based on solar, wind, and other locally available sources.

  • small scale, many sources
  • efficiency over raw power
  • designed for on-site maintenance

Water and resource cycles

Rainwater harvesting, water purification, greywater recycling, and organic matter reuse. Circularity wherever possible.

Food

Forest gardens, greenhouses, aquaponics, cooperation with local farms ‚Äî so that basic nutrition is as little dependent on global supply chains as possible.

Buildings and infrastructure

Simple, repairable structures, modular buildings, local materials, flexible spaces (work / living / education / culture).

Communication and organisation

Transparent decision-making tools, open access to information, processes built on trust instead of micromanagement.

Education and culture

The Aldea Academy ‚Äî a system of knowledge exchange between generations, artists, engineers and craftspeople. Education as a daily practice, not only a formal institution.
